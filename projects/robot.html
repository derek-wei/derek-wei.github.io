Autonomous Pet Retrieval Robot

Derek Wei, Aidan Webb, Samuel Sjoden, Noam Miller Snitz, Addison Craik

Overview

This summer, my teammates and I built a completely autonomous robot capable of line following, detecting pet stuffies with a magnetometer, and retrieving them back to safety through a zipline.On the mechanical side, our robot featured a rotating 3-joint arm with 4-wheel drive, and a bucket with two spring-loaded wheeled hooks. The electrical components of our robot were integrated into one central PCB with circuitry for ESP-32 control, power management, H-Bridges, and various sensors.

My Contributions

I took on most of the electrical design and assembly in the team. I designed circuitry for each subsystem and tested them on a breadboard before committing to a PCB. Throughout the six-week period, I made three iterations of our combined PCB in KiCad, optimizing for size and signal integrity while adding more features.

The final combined PCB featured:

  • Control board with two ESP32 microcontrollers
  • Power management from 16 V and 9 V LiPo batteries
  • Dual H-bridge motor drivers
  • Digitized line following circuitry
  • I2C bus for a magnetometer and accelerometer
  • Hysteresis filtering for rotary encoders
  • Opto-coupling between sensitive and noisy components
  • Servo and stepper motor control
